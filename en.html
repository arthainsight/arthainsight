The loop you cannot get out of

You have built something. You carry responsibility — for a business, a team, a career. You are used to solving problems on your own.

But you cannot solve this one. Every time you think it through to the end, you return to the same point. The same arguments. The same doubts. The same feeling that something essential remains unseen.

You cannot get out because you do not see what is keeping you there. Your own assumptions are the hardest to see — you do not notice them as assumptions.

More analysis will not help. More reading will not help. You need someone outside the situation who listens to you and asks the questions you do not ask yourself.

How I work

90 minutes. One topic. No slides, no tools.

You tell me your situation. I listen — not just to the words, but to what is behind them.

I ask questions. I do not comfort and I do not advise. I act as a mirror and a digger — I reflect your thoughts back to you as they are, and I dig up the assumptions you have not noticed are assumptions.

Most of the time, the essential thing is already known. It is simply covered by explanations, assumptions, or fear.

At the end of the session, you will have a clearer view of where things really stand, what you are assuming about the situation, and which thoughts are keeping you stuck. What you do with that insight is your responsibility.

Two weeks later, we return to it in a 20-minute follow-up to see what has moved.

Frequently asked questions

What does sparring at Artha Insight mean?

Sparring is a 90-minute 1:1 conversation in which we go through the situation where you are stuck. It is not coaching, mentoring, or therapy. I do not teach, advise, or give tools. My role is to help you see your own thoughts and assumptions more clearly.

Who is this sparring for?

This sparring is for entrepreneurs, leaders, and experts who are used to solving problems on their own — but some situation has started going in circles. A decision you cannot make. A direction that does not become clear. A feeling that you are doing the right things for the wrong reasons.

How are you different from a coach or business mentor?

Coaching and business mentoring build a process, provide tools, and walk alongside you for longer. I work one session at a time, with one topic. I do not move you in any direction — I do not know what is right for you. My work is to make your thinking visible to yourself.

Where does the sparring happen?

The sparring takes place remotely by video call. I work from Porvoo, but clients can be anywhere in Finland. Video makes it possible to go deep without travel.

What do I need to prepare before the session?

Nothing. It is enough that you roughly know the topic you want to discuss. You do not need to know how to phrase it perfectly — often the unformed thing is the most interesting. Reserve a quiet space for yourself and 90 minutes without interruptions.

What happens after the session?

After the session, you will have a clearer view of your situation. What you do with that insight is your responsibility — I do not follow you, assign tasks, or check your progress. Two weeks later, we return to it in a 20-minute follow-up to see what has moved.

Are you stuck?

Right now I am taking on a limited number of first clients. You do not need a perfect description of your situation. It is enough that you recognize it.
